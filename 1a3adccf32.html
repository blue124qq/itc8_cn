Improving feed efficiency and animal health through a new approach to TMR feeding

03 Aug 2020

Dairy cow rations are calculated to create a well-balanced diet. Yet, several phenomena can cause an ingested ration to differ considerably from the calculated ration. Changing feedstuffs’ characteristics due to silage management errors and environmental conditions lead to feedstuffs losing nutritional value. Loading errors lead to significant variations from the intended diet. Sorting is a third cause of ingested rations to differ considerably from calculated rations at an individual cow level. Sorting implies a selective intake of feed stuffs due to differences in taste, particle size and cows’ individual preferences. TMR (Total Mixed Rations) rations containing a variety of roughages and concentrates can create a range in particle size and taste leading to sorting, affecting feed efficiency and uniformity across a herd.
